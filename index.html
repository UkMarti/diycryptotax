Legal ways to pay less
These are real, HMRC and IRS approved strategies. Most people have never heard of them.
🇺🇸
The wash-sale loophole — sell at a loss, buy straight back
Potential saving
⌄
The IRS wash-sale rule (which prevents stocks being sold for a tax loss and immediately repurchased) does not apply to cryptocurrency. You can sell Bitcoin at a loss at 10am to harvest the tax deduction, then buy it straight back at 10:01am. You keep your position and get the tax saving.
🇬🇧
Spousal transfer — double your tax-free allowance
Up to £6,000 tax-free
⌄
In the UK, transfers between spouses or civil partners are free from Capital Gains Tax. If you’ve used your £3,000 annual allowance but your spouse hasn’t, transfer some crypto to them before selling. You both get the £3,000 allowance — effectively £6,000 tax-free between you.
🇬🇧
Negligible value claim — dead coins you still hold
Claim without selling
⌄
If a token you hold has gone to zero or been abandoned, you don’t need to sell it to claim the loss. Submit a Negligible Value Claim to HMRC. They treat the asset as sold for £0 — crystallising your capital loss on paper while you keep the token in case it ever recovers.
⛽
Gas fees reduce your taxable gain — most people miss this
Reduces your gain
⌄
Every network fee (gas fee) you paid when buying or selling crypto can be added to your cost basis or deducted from your proceeds. If you paid £50 in Ethereum gas fees to make a swap, that £50 directly reduces your taxable profit. Most people never claim these.
🇬🇧
Old losses you never reported can offset today’s gains
Up to 4 years back
⌄
Did you lose money in the 2022 crypto crash and never declare it? You have up to 4 years to register those losses with HMRC. Once registered, they carry forward indefinitely to offset future gains — meaning your 2022 losses can reduce your 2025 tax bill.
🇺🇸
HIFO accounting — sell your most expensive coins first
Minimises gains
⌄
The IRS allows “Specific Identification” for crypto. Instead of defaulting to FIFO (first coins in, first out), you can choose HIFO (highest cost first). This means when you sell, the engine picks the coins you paid the most for — mathematically minimising your capital gain every time.
